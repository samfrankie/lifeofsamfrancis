The Importance of Data Cleaning

Published on: [Date]

Portfolio | Blog

Data cleaning, also known as data wrangling, is the process of detecting and correcting (or removing) corrupt or inaccurate records from a record set, table, or database. It's often said that a data scientist spends **80% of their time** cleaning data. While this can seem tedious, it's the most critical step in the data analysis workflow.

Why is it so important?

Think of data cleaning as building the foundation of a house. If the foundation is weak, the entire structure is at risk. Similarly, a messy dataset can lead to flawed analysis, incorrect conclusions, and poor business decisions.

Common Data Issues:

  • **Missing Values**: Gaps in your data.
  • **Inconsistent Formatting**: 'NY' vs. 'New York'.
  • **Duplicate Records**: The same entry appearing multiple times.
  • **Outliers**: Data points that fall far from the rest.

By addressing these issues, you ensure that your analysis is based on accurate, reliable information.
